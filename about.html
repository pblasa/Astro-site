The Journey





In April of 1999, I packed my bags and in that year of my Saturn return at age twenty nine, I packed my bags and headed to Nepal for some adventure. It was there in a buddhist monastery in Kathmandu, that I discovered the powerful practice of meditation. One year later on a trip to Dharamsala in the buddhist enclave there called McLeod Ganj, I got to shake the Dalai Lama’s hand, and in such an omen of future destiny, I went through my first vipassana meditation retreat. It would change my life for the better. Twenty years later after the birth of a child, multiple retreats and a career in hospitality, I started my studies in astrology.

The philosophy behind evolutionary astrology, vipassana meditation go hand in hand. Astrology is the map and blueprint of where we’ve been, where we are headed, and the pitfalls that keep us from evolving as well and the gifts we bring to this life to help ourselves and others from suffering. Meditation is the action of centering ourselves in order to change the habit pattern of the mind, and therefore overcome our karmic patterns in order for us to grow and reach our potential. They both work together, for in our practice of evolutionary astrology we come to the understanding that we are fractals of the divine—we come to this life as source (god, Allah universe, or higher power) experiencing itself through desires. We return lifetime after lifetime to experience the contrast until we realize those desires are fleeting, and until all of them are exhausted until the only desire is to return to source. Through this principle, philosophy, we start understanding that all of humanity is one and the same, only each and every human is experiencing different views of the divine self. And that approach helps us to understand ourselves and to have compassion for those outside of us, to release our self-absorption and to have a clearer sympathy for those who surround us. In this understanding, we keep from harming ourselves and others, and in that action, we stop the chain of suffering, one step at a time.

In my time as a musician, graphic designer, and restaurant owner, I’ve had tests of compassion and battles to uphold a certain standard of integrity that was both able to hold and at times unable to maintain. Im like everyone taking steps backward at times, but also having the awareness to move forward. It’s all we can do.
